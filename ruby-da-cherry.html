Biography of Ruby Da Cherry

Ruby Da Cherry, born on April 22, 1990, in New Orleans, Louisiana, is an American rapper, singer, and songwriter. He is best known for being one-half of the hip-hop duo $uicideboy$, alongside his cousin $lick $loth. Growing up in a challenging environment, Ruby faced numerous obstacles but used music as an escape and a form of expression.

Music Career

Ruby Da Cherry's music career began to take shape in the early 2010s when he and $lick $loth formed $uicideboy$. The duo quickly gained popularity in the underground rap scene, known for their raw and unfiltered lyrics that often explore themes of depression, substance abuse, and existential struggles.

Early Releases

Their debut project, "Pick Your Poison," released in 2015, set the stage for their unique sound. With subsequent releases such as "Now the Moon's Rising" and "I Want to Die in New Orleans," they solidified their presence in the industry and garnered a dedicated fan base.

Mainstream Breakthrough

Ruby Da Cherry's breakthrough came with the release of their 2019 album, "I Want to Die in New Orleans," which received critical acclaim and commercial success. The album's lead single, "Paris," became a viral hit, further propelling their career into the mainstream.

Collaboration with $lick $loth

The collaboration between Ruby Da Cherry and $lick $loth is a significant aspect of their success. Their chemistry as cousins and artists allows them to create music that resonates deeply with fans. They often explore similar themes in their songs, which creates a cohesive sound throughout their discography.

Musical Style and Influences

Ruby Da Cherry's musical style is characterized by a blend of dark, atmospheric beats and introspective lyrics. Influenced by a variety of genres, including punk rock and trap, his music reflects a diverse range of inspirations.
