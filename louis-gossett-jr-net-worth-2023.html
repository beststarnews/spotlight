Early Career and Breakthrough Roles

Gossett began his acting career on stage and quickly transitioned to television and film. His breakout role came in the 1977 miniseries "Roots," where he portrayed Fiddler, a role that garnered him widespread acclaim and recognition.

After his success in "Roots," Gossett moved to film and starred in several notable projects, including "An Officer and a Gentleman," for which he won an Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor.
