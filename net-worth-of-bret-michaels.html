Biography of Bret Michaels

Bret Michaels was born on March 15, 1963, in Butler, Pennsylvania. He grew up in a musical household and was influenced by various genres, which shaped his future career. Michaels attended the Mechanicsburg Area Senior High School and later pursued music seriously, leading to the formation of Poison in the mid-1980s.

Early Career and Rise to Fame

Before achieving fame with Poison, Michaels was involved in various bands in the Pennsylvania area. His dedication and passion for music paid off when Poison was formed in 1983. The band quickly gained popularity with their debut album, "Look What the Cat Dragged In," released in 1986.

Poison's glam metal style resonated with audiences, leading to significant commercial success in the late 1980s and early 1990s. The band's hit singles, such as "Every Rose Has Its Thorn," catapulted them to stardom, earning them numerous awards and a dedicated fan base.
