Biography of Katt Williams

Katt Williams, born Micah Sierra Williams on September 2, 1971, in Cincinnati, Ohio, is an American stand-up comedian, actor, and voice actor. He is best known for his unique comedic style, which often includes social commentary and observational humor. Katt began his career performing at comedy clubs in the 1990s and quickly gained recognition for his talent.

Early Life

Katt Williams was raised in a middle-class family and showed an interest in performing arts from an early age. His parents encouraged his talent, and he began performing in school plays and local talent shows. After graduating high school, he moved to Los Angeles to pursue a career in comedy.

Career Highlights

Katt Williams' career took off in the early 2000s, with his breakout performance in the stand-up special "Katt Williams: Live: Let a Playa Play" in 2006. This special showcased his comedic genius, and he quickly became a household name.

Some of his notable works include:

  • Stand-up specials: "Katt Williams: Pimp Chronicles Pt. 1," "Kattpacalypse," and "Great America."
  • Film appearances: "Friday After Next," "Norbit," and "The Perfect Holiday."
  • Voice acting in animated series such as "The Boondocks."
